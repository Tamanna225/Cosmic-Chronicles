MARS

Mars, often called the "Red Planet," has captured human imagination as a potential destination for exploration and colonization.

  • Mass: Approximately 0.107 times that of Earth. It would take more than 6 of mars to fill the volume of Earth.
  • Diameter: 6792km
  • Temperature: Ranges from -140 to 20 degrees Celsius
  • Distance: Average distance from the Sun about 141.6 million miles
  • Gravity: About 38% of Earth's gravity. On Mars, you'd experience 62% less gravity than you're used to.
  • Speed: Orbits the Sun at about 24.077 km/s
  • Shape: Mars is an oblate spheroid, bulging slightly at the equator and flattened at the poles due to its rotation.
  • Moons: Mars has two moons, Phobos and Deimos, which are much smaller and more irregular in shape than Earth's moon.
  • Rotation Speed: Mars rotates on its axis in about 24.6 hours, which is slightly longer than a day on Earth. A day on Mars is called a sol.
  • Revolution Speed: Mars takes significantly longer to orbit the Sun than Earth. It completes one revolution every 687 Earth days (about 2 Earth years).

  • Mars is home to the largest volcano and the deepest canyon in the solar system: Olympus Mons and Valles Marineris, respectively.
  • Evidence suggests that liquid water flowed on Mars' surface in the past, indicating the potential for ancient habitability.
  • The planet's thin atmosphere is primarily composed of carbon dioxide, with traces of nitrogen and argon, hindering its ability to support life.
  • Mars has polar ice caps consisting of water and carbon dioxide ice, which may hold clues about its past climate and water history.
